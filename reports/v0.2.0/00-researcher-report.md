## 2. Uterus-Healthy Foods (Evidence-Based)

### 2.1 Top Uterus-Supporting Nutrients

Based on research from RISE Malaysia and medical literature:

1. **Omega-3 Fatty Acids** - Reduce prostaglandins (uterine contractions), anti-inflammatory
   - Sources: Ikan kembung, ikan tenggiri, ikan sardin, seeds

2. **Leafy Greens** - Alkaline balance, magnesium, potassium, vitamins
   - Sources: Pucuk ubi, midin, pucuk keledek, ulam raja

3. **Whole Grains** - Fiber flushes excess estrogen, prevents fibroids
   - Sources: Brown rice (beras perang), oats, barley (adapt traditional recipes)

4. **Probiotics** - Balanced vaginal ecosystem, pH
   - Sources: Traditional fermented foods (limited), yogurt (modern addition)

5. **Nuts & Seeds** - Omega-3, healthy fats
   - Sources: Local nuts, sesame seeds, pumpkin seeds (modern additions)

6. **Antioxidant-Rich Fruits** - Prevent fibroid growth, ovarian cancer prevention
   - Sources: Guava, berries (imported), local berries

### 2.2 Foods to AVOID for Uterus Health

| Food | Reason |
|------|--------|
| Soy products (excessive) | Phytoestrogens may stimulate cysts |
| Processed meats | Inflammatory, hormone disruption |
| Excessive red meat | Inflammatory, hormone imbalance |
| High-sugar foods | Inflammation, insulin resistance |
| Alcohol | Hormone disruption |
| Excessive caffeine | Hormone imbalance |

## 3. Diabetes-Safe Criteria (Glycemic Index Focus)

### 3.1 GI Classification for Sabah Foods

| GI Level | Range | Sabah Foods |
|----------|-------|-------------|
| **Low GI** | ≤55 | Most ulam, fish, hinava, non-starchy vegetables, guava |
| **Medium GI** | 56-69 | Ubi kayu, keledek, traditional rice (some varieties), corn |
| **High GI** | ≥70 | White rice, glutinous rice, tapai, refined noodles, sugar |

### 3.2 Blood Sugar Management Strategies

1. **Portion Control** - Even medium-GI foods OK in small portions
2. **Fiber First** - Eat ulam before carbs to slow absorption
3. **Protein Pairing** - Always pair carbs with protein (fish, chicken)
4. **Acid Addition** - Lime/vinegar in meals lowers GI impact
5. **Cooking Method** - Steamed/boiled > fried

## 4. Cultural Meal Patterns (Sabah)

### 4.1 Traditional Meal Structure

**Kadazan-Dusun:**
- **Breakfast:** Leftover rice with fish, or tapioca (tompek)
- **Lunch:** Rice, ulam, sambal, fish/meat dish
- **Dinner:** Similar to lunch, lighter portions
- **Special:** Linongot for travelers/farmers (portable meals)

**Bajau:**
- **Breakfast:** Fish, tapioca, seaweed (latok)
- **Lunch:** Rice, fresh catch, coconut-based dishes
- **Dinner:** Lighter, soups (sagol)

**Murut/Lun Bawang:**
- **Breakfast:** Nuba laya (soft rice), smoked meat
- **Lunch:** Ubi daun kayu (tapioca leaves), rice
- **Dinner:** Similar, fermented drinks (special occasions)

### 4.2 Meal Timing for Diabetes

**Recommended:**
- Breakfast: 7-8 AM (within 2 hours of waking)
- Lunch: 12-1 PM
- Dinner: 6-7 PM (at least 3 hours before sleep)
- Snacks: Only if needed (mid-morning, mid-afternoon)

**Avoid:**
- Late night eating
- Skipping meals
- Large portions at dinner

## 5. Weekly Meal Planning Framework

### 5.1 Balanced Plate Model (Bahasa Malaysia)

**"Pinggan Sihat Mak"**
- ½ Pinggan: Ulam & Sayur (Vegetables)
- ¼ Pinggan: Protein (Ikan/Ayam)
- ¼ Pinggan: Karbohidrat (Nasi Beras Perang/Ubi)

### 5.2 Sample Day (Diabetes-Safe Sabah Menu)

| Meal | Menu | Rationale |
|------|------|-----------|
| **Breakfast** | Tompek (baked tapioca) + Hinava sisa + Teh tanpa gula | Lean protein, moderate carb, no sugar |
| **Mid-Morning** | Jambu (Guava) | Low GI fruit, vitamin C |
| **Lunch** | Nasi beras perang + Pinasakan ikan + Ulam raja + Sambal tuhau | Balanced plate, anti-inflammatory |
| **Afternoon** | Ubi rebus (small) | Fiber, moderate GI |
| **Dinner** | Sagol ikan + Sayur midin goreng bawang + Nuba laya (small portion) | Light, easy to digest, elderly-friendly |

## 6. Recommendations for @architect

### 6.1 Database Schema Requirements

**Food Items Table:**
- `id`, `name_ms`, `name_english`, `local_name_sabah`
- `category` (protein/vegetable/carb/fruit/condiment)
- `diabetes_status` (safe/limit/avoid)

- `omega3_mg`, `iron_mg`, `calcium_mg`
- `antioxidant_level` (low/medium/high)
- `preparation_methods` (JSON: raw/steamed/fried/fermented)
- `cultural_origin` (Kadazan-Dusun/Bajau/Murut/Chinese)
- `availability` (year_round/seasonal)
- `notes` (text)

**Meals Table:**
- `id`, `name_ms`, `name_english`
- `meal_type` (breakfast/lunch/dinner/snack)
- `ingredients` (JSON array with quantities)
- `preparation_time_min`
- `difficulty` (easy/medium/hard)
- `diabetes_friendly` (boolean)
- `uterus_friendly` (boolean)
- `instructions_ms` (text)
- `cultural_origin`
- `image_url`

**Weekly Plans Table:**
- `id`, `user_id`, `week_start_date`
- `daily_plans` (JSON: 7 days × meals)
- `shopping_list` (JSON: aggregated ingredients)
- `total_cost_estimate`
- `nutrition_summary` (JSON: daily averages)

### 6.2 UI/UX Considerations

1. **Large Text & Buttons** - Elderly users (same as existing app)
2. **Visual Food Status** - ✅ (Boleh), ⚠️ (Kurang), ❌ (Elak)
3. **Bahasa Malaysia First** - All UI in BM
4. **Cultural Context** - Show origin (Kadazan-Dusun/Bajau/Murut)
5. **Simple Filters** - Toggle: "Semua", "Diabetes Selamat", "Rahim Sihat"
6. **Shopping List** - Auto-generate, checkbox format, market-ready

### 6.3 Integration Points

**Existing Food Guide:**
- Reuse existing food database structure
- Add meal planning fields to existing tables
- Maintain consistent status indicators (Safe/Limit/Avoid)

**Supabase:**
- Use existing auth
